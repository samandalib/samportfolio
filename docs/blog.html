Virtual Reality and Historical Preservation

Virtual reality or VR as a technology provides multiple affordances over any other accessible medium in the history of human civilization. Human beings exist and interact with the world in three dimensions, and our faculties and capabilities have evolved to suit this. Among the numerous applications that VR can be used for, education stands out as being one of the most natural and effective. We experimented with using VR to enable learning for students and other people, and chose history as a subject. History in academic and educational environments is taught mostly as information presented as a sequence of facts from an objective point. We think that it could be more effective as a first-person experience of the times, events and narratives along with an exploration of VR itself as a technology and its capability to affect and change people’s lives for the better. Towards this end, we came up with this Project.
