Desert Tourism
DESERT areas form about one third of the planet earth with lack of water, high temperature in summer and cold winter. Despite these tough conditions, they have become one of the tourist attractions of the world. From the deserts of Arizona in USA, to the Sahara in Africa and deserts of Iran and Arabia, they have all been visited by numerous tourists around the world each year. How to start thinking about providing services for tourists in desert? How can we provide required conditions for attracting tourists with a reasonable usage of energy and material and respect to the environment?

SITE


IRAN is a country located in Middle East which 70% of its lands are deserts. It has been witnessed an increasing rate of desert tourism in recent years rate.The design site is located between two desert villages in the. Mesr village, which is now a well known village in Iran, and Farahzad farm near it, as another small living district. This Location is selected because it has already been recognized and used by many tourists around the world.

.
Design Approach

a sharp and straigh line form in a soft and curvlinear context can provide a visual and spatial contrast which helps them both show themselves to the visitors sufficiently.

The Basics of Design
1- The application of passive systems to provide human comfort: to do this. the traditional Qanat system (used for reaching underground water resources) was used to create a wind tunnel on it supported by wind catchers. The cooled air is naturally directed through the living areas by the air pressure between two areas.
2- The application of eco friendly materials.
3- The application of simple building technologies: the whole structure is based on parallel load bearing walls built by rammed earth.
