House No.3 is located in Isfahan, Iran. It is located in one of the alleys of the Sheikd-sadough street. This building belongs to a three members family, a couple and their adult son. The total area of the land is almost 130 square meters and regarding the building rules of the city it can only have 80 sqm for each floor and maximum 4 floors on the ground. Besides that there was a problem with the northern neighbor that prevented locating stair box at the northern side of the land.

The Concept of Cultural Hotspot
Isfahan is an ancient city that is a musume of historical monuments from the last millenum. The concept of Cultural Hotspots suggests that designers and architects in such cities can inspire from the nearest architectural monument to design the appearance of their buildings. By doing so, every district can have a unified identity.
For this project the pigeon tower is the inspiration as the nearest cultural hotspot. at the end of the street where this project is located, there is a famous historical Pigeon Tower, where in the ancient times was build to be a place for providing piegons a home. There has been another pigeon tower near the site of this project that has been ruined in the last years. The inside appearance of these towers has been used as an inspiration point for designing the facade of the building

Interior Design
The client asked for designing the interior space of the buiding. Since the apartment is somehow small in size, the main principles in the process of interior design were:
1- Taking the maximum advantage of spaces available
2- defining private, semi-private and public spaces inside the apartment
3- bringing intimacy and peace in the interior through colors and forms
