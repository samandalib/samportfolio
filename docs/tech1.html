Locatify

Indoor Navigation | AR Mobile app

Background

We all had this experience of being in a new place and felt that we needed someone to help us find out where to go or what to do next.


Our initial concept came from our personal experiences. we visit new buildings and new places very often. It’s common that we find ourselves unable to find the room we're looking for inside a building.

Project Process

In this project, the specific goal of our research is to find solutions and ideas to help students at the University of Texas at Austin to find a room in a building easily. The original prompt was broad and we had to identify our own focus area. Through the whole process of the project, we actively engaged the target user population by conducting contextual inquiries, secondary research, prototyping and multiple rounds of usability testing to understand their pain points and needs to inform the design decisions.

3. Generative Research

In order to better understand the pain points and frustrations people have while trying to find a new place inside the campus buildings, we conducted an online survey and four contextual inquiries with UT students who are not familiar with the iSchool building.



Survey Results

We conducted an online survey and asked students to rate difficulty level of some tasks and their preferred methods of dealing with those tasks.This survey 46 respondents.



Contextual Inquiries

We conducted four contextual inquiries by accompanying our target users on their daily routine inside iSchool building, with four new UT students. By conducing the contextual inquiries, we wanted to know more about:





After the contextual inquiries, we held an interpretation session and and created an affinity diagram, to hear about the interviews and discuss what was learned. We were able to gather a series of meaningful insights through our affinity diagramming session.

Key Findings from the Contextual Inquiries

Finding #1:
Certain steps in finding a place are very difficult if not impossible without the assistance of other people.





Finding #2:
Poorly designed entrance, maze rooms and lack of signs pose the largest obstacles to people’s in the iSchool building.




Finding #3:
New students are often worried, due to starting new journey in new campuse, so they need a clear and accessible guide to find their target place easily.



User Journey Map
Based on the insights we got from the affinity diagram, we created a user journey map. We identified users’ pain points and our product’s opportunities in the entire journey.




4. Wireframe Prototype

The indoor navigation application in the first iteration of the design cycle had two parts. The first part, “Find AR content” was for users to find information about a place. The second part, “Create AR content” was for users to create AR content in the space. Once the user taps the first button, the app will ask what the user is looking for, and then users can type in the room code.

Path Vs. Target

One of the biggest differences between indoor and outdoor navigation is that all the objects are closer to the user when they are inside of a building. Hence, we believes that the best solution for interior navigation is highlighting targets for users instead of showing a path on the ground. The app highlights objects that users need to pass by with instructions in a box.

The following screens show our ideas for the process of reading AR content in the space. The flow starts with screens that aim at getting information from the users about the specific room that they are goint to find in the space. Then It directs the user step by step to the destination.

Content Creation UI flow

The following screens show our ideas for the process of creating AR content. The creation starts from the recording button in the middle of the screen, and the whole recording could be ended with the red button on the bottom left. The users can use the toolbox to add text, voice, picture, and shapes to space.


Usability Test

For the lo-fi prototype, we conducted in-person usability tests with five users and found out several usability issues.



Key Takeaways
☛ Separate the creation part from reading part. The creation part can be subject of another app.
☛ Replace technical jargon with more popular terms.
☛ Make the start of the app more simple and straight forward.
☛ Add voice command feature to make it more usable.
☛ Consider Reroute function.
☛ Make annotations and texts more clear and ordered.
☛ Highlight the destination.
