About Me


Hi!
I'm Hesam. I'm a graduate student studying UX design and HCI at UT Austin and enjoying it. Many moons ago, I started learning design principles by getting an architecture degree. I loved it, and I was good at it. After graduation, I started my journey in the creation of people's experiences in new living spaces.
Within all the time I worked as an architect, I learned how to be an effective member of a multidisciplinary team. I learned communication skills in practice and how to manage the quality of projects that might take years to finish. I worked and learned a lot of things that built up my assets for the rest of my life and I'm revising and updating my findings every single minute of my life.
As computer technology grew up and new hardware and software were available at affordable prices for every individual in any industry, the workflow started to change in architecture practice as well as many others. My curiosity and passion for learning new tools and technologies pushed me toward learning programming. I couldn't believe that I would love it that much! The more I learned, the more thirsty I became. It was the beginning of my internal movement that took me to where I am now.
I love creation. It requires design, methodology, tools, skills, and management of course. It did not take me too much time to get to the concept of User Experience Design. I found it an intersection that my past, ambitions, and future can meet. Nothing seems more interesting than creating a user-centered product, that is novel and benefits from technological advancements.
Now I am at the beginning of my journey and there is a lot that I love to learn and experience.

Feel free to contact me at handalib@utexas.edu and I will be more than happy to hear from you.
