Biomimicry


First Prize Winner at Biomimicry Design Challenge 2011, USA
.

The Challenge
About one third of the planet earth consists of arid areas with lack of water, high temperature in summer and cold winter. The growing population of the world and the need to live in tough conditions emphasize on looking for a way to cope with these conditions. The question is that how to start thinking about life in desert? It can be replied with a simple question: is human being the first who want to live there? Of course not! However, the current conditions of the universe requires seeking for more sustainable methods toward making life easy in such conditions.

What type of life can be a good choice to investigate?
Among all these, we focused on the snaildue to its amazing form and mechanism which makes its life possible in a quite architectural way, rather than others using mostly biological procedures to survive.

.

Evaluation of the Form
We have to look for shadow in desert. The sunlight beams make every object overheated in desert and the best and cheapest way is to make shadow. In order to do that, the shells were formed and investigated in various positions to reach to the most efficient shading of the form. The shells can provide shade for each other and the whole building as well, as the spiral shell of the snail do. The purpose of design is to make human comfort zone by minimizing the solar radiation besides moisturizing and providing ventilation.

Final Form
By taking biology into design the resulted concept would be of its context not far from it. Taking living creatures such as snail as the inspirational object would lead us towards finding more sustainable solutions and will omit the costs of nonconformance we use to pay due to living far from the nature.

Energy Analysis
The designed form is analysed in Autodesk Ecotect software to investigate its performance against the typical buildings in desert areas. This analyze was praised by Autodest and published on Autodesk Sustainability Workshop Webpage.
